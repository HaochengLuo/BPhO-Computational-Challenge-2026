# BPhO-Computational-Challenge-2026

## Introduction

This repository contains my code for the **British Physics Olympiad (BPhO) Computational Challenge 2026**.

The 2026 challenge theme is **Quantum Mechanics**. The models are built in **Python** and focus on simulation, plotting, animation, and visualisation of the challenge tasks.

## Task Overview

1. Random walk
2. Brownian motion
3. Planck black-body spectrum and Einstein heat-capacity model
4. Photoelectric effect
5. Hydrogen emission spectrum and Bohr model
6. Electron diffraction
7. Particle in a box
8. Quantum cryptography mismatch probabilities
9. Compton scattering
10. Hydrogenic orbitals

## Solutions

- `task01/main.py` - random walk with `N` steps of size `s`.
- `task02/main.py` - Brownian motion with random small particles and momentum-conserving collisions with a large particle.
- `task03/main.py` - Planck black-body spectra and Einstein heat capacity model.
- `task04/main.py` - photoelectric stopping voltage for different metals.
- `task05/main.py` - hydrogen emission spectrum from Bohr model energy levels.
- `task06/main.py` - electron diffraction rings and straight-line voltage check.
- `task07/main.py` - particle-in-a-box energy levels, probability densities, and uncertainty check.
- `task08/main.py` - classical and quantum polarization mismatch probability calculator.
- `task09/main.py` - Compton wavelength shift, electron recoil speed, and recoil angle.
- `task10/main.py` - hydrogenic orbital 2D slices and 3D probability-density visualization.
